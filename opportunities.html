Making it easier to get involved

Finding VR projects to get involved with should not be hard. Here's a curated list of projects looking for developers like you to help advance the field. Check them out!

Featured Opportunites

Animate Objects

Animate Objects/AO is a new San Diego startup that creates Augmented Reality experiences for theme parks, attractions, events and products. The company is founded by one of San Diego's most well known startup entrepreneurs who is the organizer for the San Diego Augmented Reality User Group and also created the largest Kickstarter campaign in SD history with $928k for "The Pocket Drone." At one point in his career he also worked for the Academic Computing Dept at UCSD. Animate Objects/AO is currently working with major parks and museums to bring our location based augmented reality ecosystem to launch as early as this summer. They are in need of Unity content creators, animators, scripters and 3D modelers who will help make unique experiences for our platform and customers. Please send resume and links to any available work, screenshots and whatever else you feel is relevant to info@animateobjects.net

NASA

The Jet Propulsion Laboratory is taking applications for interns interested in working with Virtual/Augmented Reality. They are working to provide AR training simulations for astronauts in space as well as visualizations for real time missions on Mars. Technologies they work with include mobile imaging and Microsoft’s HoloLens. You can find more information about JPL’s OpsLab here: http://opslab.jpl.nasa.gov/. If you are interested, send your resume/portfolio to OpsLab member Victor Luo at Victor.Luo@jpl.nasa.gov and mention you are a part of Virtual Reality Club at UC San Diego.

Engineers for Exploration

Engineers for Exploration is currently seeking undergraduate interns to assist in creating technologies for archaeologists, environmentalists, explorers etc. There are several ongoing projects including GUI design and experimentation with 3d reconstruction algorithms and devices (using Google Tango tablets). If this sounds interesting you should apply. Engineers for Exploration also has a 10-week summer paid internship available which you can find more information about here.

Internship opportunity at Intel

Intel's VR team is looking for experienced C# programmers for a full time position in Folsom, CA. Please send your resume and github to djusto@ucsd.edu for more information

DataViz research project at UCSD's DesignLab

Professor Scott Klemmer is looking for VR undergraduate students to develop data visualization tools using Unity. Please send your resume and github to djusto@ucsd.edu for more information

Psychology research project at Tu's lab

Professor Tu is looking to study the effects of long term exposure to virtual enviroments. This is the first research of its kind and will require experienced programmers with 3D modeling experienced to create a photo-realistic virtual reality experience to use a research tools. Please send your resume and github to djusto@ucsd.edu for more information

Internship opportunity at Coursera

Coursera's team is developing their experimental VR platform. They're looking into incorporating google cardboard to their classes to further test how to develop intuition on abstract mathematical concepts better. You will be working in the experimental user experience team in Seattle, WA. Looking exclusively for seniors. Please send your resume and github to djusto@ucsd.edu for more information
